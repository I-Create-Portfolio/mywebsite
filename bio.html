Bio

GORDWIN ODHIAMBO (b.1993) is a Kenyan contemporary photographer and artist born and raised in Nairobi, Kenya. His photography and life is shaped mostly by the works he has done in Kibera slums. It explores a peoples' determination and their inextricable connection to where they live, the plights faced in trying to navigates the confines and struggles of urban life, and the endearing intricasies of the everyday life in the community.

His practice involves visiting a place many times until he sees the changes in real time.

Gordwin has been part of journalism fellowships including DW Akademie. He has attended several advanced photojournalism masterclasses with VII Photo Academy, NOOR Images, World Press Photo Foundation among others. In 2024, he was part of the New York Times portfolio (Photography)review.

Assignments include with Bloomberg, National Geographic, NYTIMES, Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation, Acumen, Open Society Foundation among others.

In 2019, he founded, and he's currently the Director of Kibera Documentary Center
